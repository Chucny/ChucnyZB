ChucnyZB

ChucnyZB is a variation of the ZB method (Zborowski-Bruchem method) that incorporates a specialized slotting technique called ChucnySlot and a subset of ZBLL algorithms known as 2GLL. It was developed to streamline the slot insertion/solving and last layer for improved efficiency.

Overview

ChucnyZB combines classic CFOP solving steps with targeted enhancements:

  1. Solve the Cross.
  2. F2L → insert the last slot using ChucnySlot, which orients edges and permutes corners while inserting the last slot. OR solve slot by inserting the pieces to the slot (no matter orientation) and then solve them AND permute corners and orient edges (83 algorithms).
  3. Finish the last layer using a ZBLL subset called 2GLL (2 Generator Last Layer) to complete permutation of the last layer in fewer moves.
  • Average moves: 47
  • Number of algorithms: 103 + (optional 47)
  • ChucnySlot algorithms (pair up): 48
  • Other ChucnySlot algorithms: 47

Steps

  1. Cross – Make a cross
  2. F2L – Solve F2L + ChucnySlot
  3. ZBLL – Use 2GLL algorithms to solve the last layer, mostly using 2-generator algorithms

Note that the alternative method is NOT recommended since it’s not finished yet.

How It Works

The key idea behind ChucnyZB is to solve the last slot by inserting with one of 48 ChucnySlot algorithms, permuting the corners and orienting the edges while solving/inserting the last slot on the right side to force 2GLL. Basically, you sacrifice speed for efficiency.

Other variation: Insert two F2L pieces to the slot and solve EO and CP while solving the pieces. If free slot, use ChucnySlot (or VLS if easy case).
